Depression

7 tools to help manage your depression right now

  • Just do it
    • Do pleasant things that are possible right now.
  • Mastery
    • Do things that make you feel competent and effective to combat helplesness and hopelessness.
  • The lifestyle
    • Do this now: Eat, exercise, sleep and avoid mood aaltering substances. How do you feel?
  • Avoid Avoidance
    • Don't wait to accept the way you're feeling. Tell someone how you're feeling right away so you can immediately start to move through it. We know it doesn't magically disappear but it will fade because it always does and you know this.
  • Be your own protector
    • Monitor and protect your self-esteem. Now is when you're probably feeling your worst and putting yourself down. Take a moment to be compassionate to yourself. Imagine a friend is feeling bad about themselves, what would you say to them?
  • Look forward
    • Look past how you are currently feeling. What would you like to acheive in the next few weeks? What are you looking forward to? What is something you'd like to get better at? Can you take some time to help someone in the next few weeks? Who needs you?
  • Get present
    • Take ten slow, deep breaths. Focus on breathing out as slowly as possible until the lungs are completely empty and then allow them to refill by themselves. Notice the sensations of your lungs emptying and refilling. This is all there is to living.
